B.K.S. Iyengar

Who is B.K.S. Iyengar?

Bellur Krishnamachar Sundararaja(B.K.S.) Iyengar is one of the most influential yoga teachers of all kind. Dubbed such names as "the Michelangelo of Yoga” and the “king of yogis,” Iyengar was credited for bringing yoga to the West (Iyengar Yoga National Association of the U.S, para 1, 2017). B.K.S. Iyengar was born on December 14, 1918 in Bellur, India (Ramanani Iyengar Memorial Yoga Institute, n.a.; Busia, 2010). Mr. Iyengar suffered much adversities in his youth such as suffering from poverty, sickness and the death of his father at the age of eight and a half years old (Busia, 2010). In 1934, when Mr. Iyengar was 16, he was sent to study yoga with his brother-in-law, Krishnamacharya (Goldberg, 2014). Krishnamacharya is considered one of the key figures in the revival of the physical practice of yoga, hatha yoga (Mohan, n.a.). Training with Krishnamacharya was extremely demanding for Iyengar and he started to use props in order to ensure proper alignment and to prevent injury (Goldberg, 2014). This emphasis on alignment and the use of props would soon become the defining characteristics of Iyengar Yoga.
